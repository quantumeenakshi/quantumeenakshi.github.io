About Me

I am an experimental physicist with deep experience in translating fundamental quantum physics into emerging applications in sensing and quantum information science.

My research focuses on uncovering novel quantum phenomena in low-dimensional superconductors, probing Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) transitions, phase slips, and topological fluctuations. I utilize a comprehensive suite of methodologies: from design and device modeling to advanced nanofabrication and precision cryogenic transport measurements.

Currently, as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Chemical Physics of Solids (MPI-CPFS), I collaborate with Dr. Uri Vool's group to characterize superconducting resonators and optimize disordered NbN films for next-generation quantum circuits.
